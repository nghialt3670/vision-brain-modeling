# EEG

> Electroencephalography — low-cost, high-temporal-resolution neural recording for real-time brain decoding.

---

## How EEG Works

EEG measures **electrical potentials** on the scalp generated by the synchronized activity of large populations of neurons:

1. Post-synaptic potentials in cortical pyramidal neurons generate electrical dipoles.
2. Scalp electrodes detect the summed potential from thousands of aligned neurons.
3. Signals are amplified and digitized at high sampling rates (e.g., 250–2000 Hz).

## Technical Specifications

| Property | Typical Value |
|----------|--------------|
| Spatial resolution | ~cm (scalp surface) |
| Temporal resolution | ~ms |
| Sampling rate | 250–2000 Hz |
| Number of channels | 14 (consumer) to 256+ (research) |
| Setup time | 10–60 minutes |
| Cost | $500–$50,000 |

---

## Strengths

- **Millisecond temporal resolution** — captures rapid perceptual events.
- **Low cost** and widely available.
- **Portable** — compatible with BCI and real-world use.
- High sampling rate enables time-frequency analysis.
- Sensitive to early visual processing (~100–200 ms post-stimulus).

---

## Limitations

- **Poor spatial resolution** due to volume conduction through skull and scalp.
- Susceptible to ocular (eye blink/movement) and muscle artifacts.
- Low Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR) — averaging many trials often required.
- Cannot directly image deep subcortical structures.
- High inter-session and inter-subject variability.

| Component | Latency | Interpretation |
|-----------|---------|---------------|
| **P1** | ~100 ms | Early visual cortex response (V1) |
| **N170** | ~170 ms | Face-selective response (FFA) |
| **P300** | ~300 ms | Cognitive relevance / target detection |
| **N400** | ~400 ms | Semantic incongruity |
| **Gamma oscillations** | 40–100 Hz | High-level feature binding |

## EEG in Brain Decoding Pipelines

### Feature Extraction from EEG

- **Epoching**: Extract fixed-length windows time-locked to stimulus onset (e.g., 0–1000 ms).
- **ERP features**: Average over trials; compute amplitude at specific components (P1, N170).
- **PSD features**: Power spectral density in theta, alpha, beta, gamma bands.
- **Time-frequency features**: Short-time Fourier transform (STFT) or wavelet decomposition.
- **Learned features**: Feed raw or filtered epochs into CNN/Transformer encoders.

### Common EEG Encoder Architectures

| Architecture | Notes |
|-------------|-------|
| EEGNet | Lightweight CNN; widely used baseline |
| ShallowConvNet / DeepConvNet | Standard EEG classification backbones |
| EEG-Conformer | CNN + Transformer hybrid |
| NICE (2023) | Masked autoencoding on EEG for decoding |
| ATM (2024) | EEG → CLIP space mapping for zero-shot reconstruction |
