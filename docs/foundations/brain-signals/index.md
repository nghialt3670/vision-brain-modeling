# Brain Signals

---

## Definition

Brain signals are measurements of neural activity produced when the brain processes information, perceives the environment, or performs actions. Since neurons communicate through electrical and chemical processes, these activities can be recorded using specialized devices and converted into signals for computational analysis.

Different recording technologies capture different aspects of brain activity and provide different trade-offs between spatial resolution, temporal resolution, cost, and invasiveness.

---

## Modalities

| Modality | Measures | Spatial Resolution | Temporal Resolution | Cost | Invasiveness |
|----------|----------|-------------------|--------------------|------|-------------|
| [**fMRI**](/foundations/brain-signals/fmri/) | Blood oxygen changes (BOLD) | High | Low | Very high | Non-invasive |
| [**EEG**](/foundations/brain-signals/eeg/) | Electrical activity | Low | High | Low | Non-invasive |
| [**MEG**](/foundations/brain-signals/meg/) | Magnetic fields | High | High | Very high | Non-invasive |
| [**fNIRS**](/foundations/brain-signals/fnirs/) | Blood oxygen changes | Medium | Low | Moderate | Non-invasive |
| [**PPG**](/foundations/brain-signals/ppg/) | Blood volume pulse | None (peripheral) | High | Very low | Non-invasive |
| [**ECoG**](/foundations/brain-signals/ecog/) | Electrical activity on cortex | Very High | Very High | Very high | Invasive |
