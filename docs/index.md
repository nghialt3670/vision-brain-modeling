# Vision-Brain Modeling

> A living knowledge base for Vision-Brain Modeling

Vision-Brain Modeling is an interdisciplinary field that investigates the relationship between visual perception and brain activity. It aims to understand how the brain encodes visual information and how computational models can capture, predict, and interpret these neural representations.

By combining neuroscience, computer vision, and machine learning, researchers develop models that bridge visual stimuli and neural responses. These models provide insights into the mechanisms of human vision while enabling applications such as neural decoding, brain-computer interfaces, and brain-inspired artificial intelligence.

The field spans multiple brain recording modalities, including fMRI, EEG, MEG, fNIRS, and ECoG, and leverages advances in deep learning and foundation models to study visual processing at increasingly large scales.

This knowledge base provides a structured overview of the concepts, methodologies, datasets, benchmarks, and research developments that define the rapidly evolving landscape of Vision-Brain Modeling.
